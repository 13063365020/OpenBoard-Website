About OpenBoard

OpenBoard is an open source cross-platform interactive whiteboard application designed primarily for use in schools and universities. It can be used both with interactive whiteboards or in a dual-screen setup with a pen-tablet display and a beamer.

It was originally forked from Open-Sankoré, which was itself based on Uniboard.

Cross-platform

Supported platforms are Windows (7+), OS X (10.9+) and Linux (tested on Ubuntu 14.04 and 16.04).

Who is involved?

Currently, OpenBoard is maintained by the Education Department (DIP) of the Canton of Geneva, in Switzerland. The University of Lausanne, who developped the original Uniboard software in 2003 has recently joined the effort.

Interest is growing fast across Switzerland but also in Europe and North America. A swiss law non-profit organisation is to be created shortly. This organisation will promote the open source development of OpenBoard and foster collaboration of interested parties.
